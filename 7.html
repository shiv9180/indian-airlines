Air India is the flag carrier of India, owned by the Tata Group and Singapore Airlines. Founded as Tata Airlines in 1932 by J.R.D. Tata, it currently operates an extensive network of domestic and international routes across five continents.
Key Information
1.Ownership: The airline is primarily owned by the Tata Group (74.9%), with Singapore Airlines holding a 25.1% stake. It was renationalized in 1953 and returned to the Tata Group in January 2022 after 69 years.
2.Hubs: Its main hub is at the Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, with secondary hubs in Mumbai and Bengaluru.
3.Destinations: As of November 2025, Air India serves 87 destinations, including 42 domestic and 45 international locations
. 4.Fleet: The fleet consists of a mix of narrow-body and wide-body Airbus and Boeing aircraft, including A320 family jets, A350s, Boeing 777s, and 787 Dreamliners, suitable for both short-haul and long-haul flights.
5.Alliance: Air India has been a member of Star Alliance, the largest global airline consortium, since July 2014.
6.Subsidiary: Air India Express is its low-cost subsidiary, focusing on affordable short and medium-haul international routes and domestic flights. The merger of Vistara with Air India was completed in November 2024, consolidating the Tata Group's full-service airline business.
Transformation
Since returning to the Tata Group, Air India has embarked on a five-year transformation program called 'Vihaan.ai'. This initiative includes a record-breaking order for 470 new aircraft, a $400 million refurbishment plan for existing planes, and a complete overhaul of services and technology to become a world-class global airline.
